Mapping Australia's Education & Employment Gap

How do location, education, and industry shape employment opportunities across Australia? A visual investigation across states, regions, qualifications, and industries.

01 - Labour Market Overview

Where unemployment hits hardest

Starting from a national view, we move from state averages to the time dimension, and then down to the regional level - where the real disparities live.

Snapshot · Dec 2025

State and Territory Unemployment

Victoria records the highest unemployment rate and the ACT the lowest, but most states sit within a narrow band. State averages alone hide where labour market pressure is actually concentrated.

Trend · 2010-2025

Australia's Unemployment Over Time

From 2010 to 2025, unemployment fluctuated rather than declined smoothly. The COVID-19 shock pushed the rate above 6%, before falling to a 15-year low in 2023 and edging up again.

Map · Interactive · Dec 2025

Regional Unemployment Across Australia

Zooming into SA2 regions reveals what state averages smooth over: unemployment is often concentrated in specific local pockets, with neighbouring regions showing very different rates. Use the state filter to explore local variation.

02 - Workforce Activity

Beyond unemployment alone

A single rate rarely tells the full story. Participation, employment-to-population, and underemployment add the texture needed to understand workforce strength.

Trend · 2016-2026

Labour Force Participation

Participation rose from 64.9% in March 2016 to 66.8% in March 2026 - more Australians are active in the labour market today, adding important context to the unemployment story.

Comparison · Dec 2025

Employment Indicators by State

A state's labour market strength looks different depending on the indicator. Comparing unemployment, participation, employment-to-population, and underemployment together gives a fuller picture than any single rate.

03 - Education Profile

An uneven map of qualifications

Before examining how education connects to employment, we look at how it is distributed across the country.

Snapshot · 2025

Bachelor Degree Attainment by State and Territory

Bachelor-degree attainment ranges from 46.3% in the ACT down to 27.3% in Tasmania - a gap of nearly 20 percentage points. University-level qualifications are far from evenly distributed across Australia.

04 - Education & Employment

How qualifications shape outcomes

The link between what you've studied and how you participate in the workforce is striking - and it widens at the lower end of the qualification ladder.

Snapshot · 2025

Labour Force Status by Qualification Type

Higher qualifications link clearly to stronger workforce attachment: 61.0% of bachelor degree holders work full-time, versus just 33.0% of those without a non-school qualification. The latter group is also 2.5 times more likely to be outside the labour force entirely (38.1% vs 15.5%).

05 - Industry Opportunities

Where the jobs and the dollars are

Industry choice shapes opportunity through four lenses: size, pay, growth, and hiring pressure - and they rarely move in the same direction.

Aug 2025 · Top 10 industries

Industry Employment vs Median Weekly Earnings

The largest employers are not the highest payers. Health Care and Social Assistance leads in workforce size but pays slightly below the $1,741 all-industries median, while Professional, Scientific and Technical Services sits well above it.

Change · Aug 2024 - Aug 2025

Employment Change Across the 10 Largest Industries

Health Care and Social Assistance added roughly 117,700 workers and Professional Services added 87,400 - service-based industries drove most of the growth. Manufacturing, by contrast, lost around 28,500 jobs.

Aug 2025

Industry Workforce Size vs Job Vacancy Pressure

Workforce size doesn't equal hiring demand. Administrative and Support Services shows the highest vacancy pressure (around 63 per 1,000 employed) despite being far smaller than Health Care, which has many vacancies in absolute terms but only modest pressure relative to its size.

Multi-indicator · 2025

Industry Opportunity Profile: Earnings, Vacancy Pressure & Workforce Size

No single industry leads on all three measures. Professional Services tops earnings, Health Care tops workforce size, and Accommodation and Food Services tops vacancy pressure - so "best opportunity" depends entirely on what you're optimising for.

So, what shapes employment opportunities in Australia?

Opportunity in Australia is shaped by three intersecting forces: location (where local labour markets sit), education (which qualifications open which doors), and industry (where pay, growth, and hiring demand actually concentrate). No single indicator captures the full picture - it's the combination that matters.
